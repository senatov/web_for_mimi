Finder still can’t do dual-panel. I fixed it.

I never found a file manager on macOS that didn’t make me want to throw the laptop. Finder has no dual pane. Forklift costs money and still feels like a wrapper. Commander One wants a subscription for SFTP. So I built my own. MiMiNavigator gives you two panels, tabs, favorites, history, FTP/SFTP — and opens ZIP, RAR, 7Z, TAR, GZIP, BZIP2, XZ, ISO, CAB, XAR, LZH, ARJ, CPIO like folders. Nested archives too. It’s free, it’s signed, and I’m still shipping fixes every week.

Why does this exist?

Because after 20 years of macOS, Apple still thinks one panel is enough. I grew up on Total Commander, moved to Mac, and nothing came close. So I wrote my own.

What’s it actually good at?

Moving files without losing your mind. Two panels, tabs, SFTP that just works, 15+ archive formats as folders, and a keyboard workflow that doesn’t fight you.

How do I get it?

Grab the signed DMG above. Mount, drag to Applications, done. No Gatekeeper drama, no App Store middleman. Source is on GitHub if you’re curious.
